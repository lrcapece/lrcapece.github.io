About me.

Coastal Scientist · Biogeochemist · Associate, Silvestrum Climate Associates

I am a coastal scientist and biogeochemist with nearly a decade of experience studying marine and coastal ecosystems, specializing in blue carbon across tidal marshes, mangroves, and seagrass systems.

I am currently an Associate in Blue Carbon Science and Implementation at Silvestrum Climate Associates, where I work at the intersection of rigorous science and real-world conservation outcomes — translating research into actionable strategies for carbon markets, ecosystem monitoring, and coastal restoration.

I completed my PhD in Earth Science at UC Santa Barbara in the Raven NOISE Lab, where my doctoral research investigated sulfur-carbon interactions in blue carbon ecosystems including mangroves and salt marshes. Prior to that, I earned my MSc in Geology from UC Davis in the Ocean Climate Lab, studying carbon sequestration in California seagrass meadows.

My work spans international fieldwork coordination, stakeholder engagement, multi-institutional collaboration, and science communication. I am skilled in geospatial analysis, statistical modeling, and large-scale data workflows using R, Python, ArcGIS Pro, and QGIS.

Outside of work, you'll find me running, surfing, skiing, or cooking.
